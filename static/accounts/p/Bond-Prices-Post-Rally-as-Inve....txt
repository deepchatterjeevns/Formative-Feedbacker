Bond Prices Post Rally as Investors Gain Renewed Confidence Rates Will Decline
NEW YORK -- Bond prices rallied strongly yesterday amid renewed investor optimism that interest rates will decline in coming months.</br></br>President Reagan's call for a special prosecutor to investigate the Iran arms sale helped bolster investor confidence, according to analysts. They said the action eased fears, especially among foreign investors, that the scandal would hamper the administration in carrying out fiscal and economic policy.</br></br>Prices of some Treasury bonds spurted about three-quarters of a point, or around $7.50 for each $1,000 face amount of securities. Investor optimism spilled over into other financial markets. In the stock market, the Dow Jones Industrial Average surged more than 43 points to close at 1955.57. More than 230 million shares changed hands on the New York Stock Exchange.</br></br>The U.S. dollar rose against most foreign currencies, snapping a string of several declines. Prices of precious metals plunged amid profit-taking from recent rallies.</br></br>Investors appear confident that a special prosecutor "would help clear the air more quickly of the Iranian problem," said Richard B. Hoey, chief economist at Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc. Arnold X. Moskowitz, senior vice president and economist at Dean Witter Reynolds Inc., agreed that investors seem to be "giving the president the benefit of the doubt" by "putting aside" the Iran issue in their investment decisions.
